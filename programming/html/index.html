HTML stands for HyperText Markup Language, and as you can guess, is a markup language.

HTML consists of Elements. Elements have a tag, and they can also have attributes.
Let's take a look at a common element, a line break.
<br>
(note that in order to display that on the webpage, I had to use ASCII character codes instead of actual <s and >s. The ASCII codes are:
< = &lt;
> = &gt;)

A line break is a very simple element. It simply acts as if you hit the enter, or return, key on your keyboard.
The <br> is very important, since html doesn't recognize a line break in the code.
Next up, another commonly used element, the <p> element.
The <br> is just a marker, however, the <p> actually has a body. So, instead of simply putting <br>, you have to put <p> elements like this:

<p> </p>

Why would you want to do this? Why, to put text into it of course!
The "p" stands for paragraph, so we are going to call this paragraph text.
Let's look at a simple example, using both of the things that we just learned.


                    <br>

                    <p>Hello there! My name is Salt, what's yours?</p>

                    <p>Cool name!</p>

                    <br>

                    <br>
